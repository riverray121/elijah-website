FULL ROOM LOFT


In New York City, housing is extremely small and very expensive. To combat this, I built a loft that covers my entire floor space in my bedroom, doubling my usable space for no added rent.

Finished Product

9x8ft room transformed into a lofted platform with a queen bed and storage up top and a desk, futon, and storage down below.
Fiber optic star ceiling is unnecessary but awesome.

Bare Bones Stucture

I built the loft entirely within my 9x8ft room. This included all cuts, assembly, and design adjustments.
One issue I did not anticipate was the way my cheap circular saw and the poor quality of New York lumber
would result in an enormous amount of smoke as I cooked each 2x4 I cut far past well done.

CAD Initial Mock-Up

After getting the dimensions of the room I played around with various layouts and designs until I was happy.
